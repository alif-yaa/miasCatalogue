Description
الوصف
Written after a vision in 595H, this work is devoted to bringing out the role of the mahdī – the “rightly guided leader” – and the Seal of the Saints (whom he identifies with Jesus), discussing both a prophetic aspect which looks to the unfolding of their functions in time, and a mystical aspect which understands them as realities which can be realised in the heart of the complete human being.
Manuscripts
مخطوطات
Verified by at least four historic mss. The best is Berlin Oct. 3266 (described by Gerald Elmore in Islamic Sainthood in the Fullness of Time (Leiden, 1999) pp. 197-199), which was written in 597H, and is the oldest known ms. of IA’s. Also excellent is Private 1, written in 637H by a well-known disciple, Muḥammad b. ʿAbd al-Qādir al-Anṣāri, in Damascus and checked against the original in IA’s hand; however, according to Abrar Ahmed Shahi, who has prepared a critical edition of the text, this mss contains only about 50% of the work. Another early copy, dated 707H in the Turkish catalogue (http://ktp.isam.org.tr/), is Veliyuddin 1743 ff.1-79. Another copy, dated 834H, in Milli Hk 15 ff.12b-43b, is available in digital reproduction here: https://www.yazmalar.gov.tr/detay_goster.php?k=77129 JC notes that most of the poems from ʿAnqāʾ mughrib are also found as a block – and as complete poems – in the Dīwān, p. 26-33.
