Description
The most famous and widely read of all Ibn ʿArabī’s works, containing the epitome of his teachings on the meaning of the perfect human being (al-insān al-kāmil). It takes the form of 27 chapters on the prophets from Adam to Muhammad, each associated with a knowledge; for example; the wisdom on divinity in the word of Adam: the wisdom of light in the word of Joseph. Ibn ʿArabī’s intention in the Fuṣūṣ is to draw out the interior, saintly wisdom of each prophet rather than the religious law which they brought.

Manuscripts
مخطوطات
A much copied and commented on work, verified by at least seven historic mss. The best is Evkaf Muzesi 1933, which is SQ’s riwāya. written in his hand in 630H, with two samāʿ in IA’s hand. Other early mss: Kastamonu Hk 2024 (710H), Paris 1340 (726H), Corum Hk 3909 (728H), Konya Ak 308 (757H)

Editions
Beirut edition edited by Abu ʿAlā ʿAfīfī (Beirut, nd) is based upon several mss. but did not use the Ṣādr al-dīn al-Qūnawī autograph (Evkaf Musezi 1933). A new critical edition using the Qūnawī autograph, plus a translation into Urdu, by the Ibn ʿArabī Foundation (Rawalpindi, Pakistan). Ed. Abrār Aḥmad Shāhī. Critical edition by ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz al-Manṣūb (with Abrār Aḥmad Shāhī) (Cairo 2016) based on mss. from the MIAS archive. A critical edition alongside Naqsh al-Fuṣūṣ, with complete references to Qurʾān and ḥadith by Dr Saiyad Nizamuddin al-Husayni (Cairo, date unknown). Critical edition by Mahmud Kiliç and Abdurrahim Alkiṣ (Istanbul 2016), fully vowelled based upon al-Kashānī’s commentary plus a facsmile of the oldest ms, Evkaf Muzesi 1933, written by Ṣadr al-dīn al-Qūnawi.
