Description
Written in answer to a request from a friend and companion that he should explain the journey of ascension to the ‘lord of power’ and the return to the creatures. It describes the spiritual quest in terms of a non-stop ascension through the various levels of existence and knowledge leading to the level of human perfection.

Manuscripts
مخطوطات
A very commonly copied work in the early collections, verified by at least four historic mss. The best is probably Yusuf Aga 7838, which is from the SQ’s library, with a samāʿ dated 651H in Aleppo in SQ’s hand. Often confused with another treatise on retreat, K. al-Khalwa (RG255) (as OY has done with Yusuf Aga 7838 copy). Other mss listed: Dar al-Kutub: dated 602H in collection 23 maj. (http://www.manuscripts.idsc.gov.eg/Manuscript/Manuscript_OPAC/Availability.aspx?ID=5323&ResID=1) and dated 600H is in collection 31 maj. (http://www.manuscripts.idsc.gov.eg/Manuscript/Manuscript_OPAC/Availability.aspx?ID=5544&ResID=8) - dating almost certainly drawn from dates in text not the date of composition.
