Description
Stories of Dhū al-Nūn (d. 180/796), the famous early Egyptian Sufi master, recorded by IA from various companions. Written after the death of Yūnus b. Yaḥyā (d. 608H), who is referred to as no longer alive, and perhaps composed in Cairo where Dhū al-Nūn is buried.

Manuscripts
مخطوطات
Not listed in OY, and only one ms. in the Topkapi library is extant. Its acceptance as a verified text by IA comes from the internal evidence of the people cited in the text (Stephen Hirtenstein). There are two printed Arabic editions; one by ʿAbd al-Fattāh in Rasāʾil (Vol 3) and one by Ibrahīm al-Kayyālī (Beirut 2005).
