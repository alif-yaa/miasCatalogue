Description
Commentary by IA himself on his cycle of love poetry, Tarjumān al-Ashwāq (RG 767), bringing out the esoteric meaning of the verses. As IA explains in the preface, he wrote it at the request of Badr al-Ḥabashī and Ibn Sawdakīn in Aleppo in 611H because it had incited the disapproval of some of the local ʿulamāʾ.

Manuscripts
مخطوطات
A much copied work, verified by two historic mss., Evkaf Muzesi 1713 (873H) and Fatih 5322 (934H), which are both copied from originals written by Ayyūb b. Badr, with samāʿ dated 626H and 616H resp. Most mss. also include the full text of the Tarjuman (RG 767), of which there are also some separate copies. Copy in Baghdad, Iraq Museum 11086/2 which a cataloguer believed was copied in the 7th century (al-Furqān online: https://www.al-furqan.com/our_is_item/manid/1250158/groupid/139348) See: مخطوطات الأدب في المتحف العراقي = Makhṭūṭāt al-adab fī al-Matḥaf al-ʿIrāqī = Catalogue of Manuscripts of Literature In the Iraq Museum Library, p.444 (#1155). After transcribing the usual text in which IA gives the reason he wrote the work the catalogue has this: فرغ منه المؤلف سنة 614H بمدرسة الامير مظفر الدين محمود This information does not occur in most mss: completed 614H in the madrasah of Amīr Muẓaffar al-dīn Maḥmūd. (It occurs in the Shuqayrī edition of 1995).

Editions
The 1911 translation of Tarjumān al-ashwāq by Nicholson (London, 1911) contains a critical edition of the poems and the introduction, but not of the commentary. Critical edition by Dr Muḥammed ʿAlam al-Dīn al-Shuqayrī (Cairo, 1995) Critical edition and German translation in preparation by Georg Bossong of the University of Zurich.
طبعات
Translations
Partially translated into English by R H Nicholson alongside his translation of the poems in The Tarjumān al-ashwāq, London, 1911. Fully translated into French by Maurice Gloton alongside his translation of the poems in L’interprète des désirs, Paris, 1996.
