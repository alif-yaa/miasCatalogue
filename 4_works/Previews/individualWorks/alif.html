Description
الوصف
Treatise on the unity symbolically represented by the letter Alif. Alternative title is K. al-Aḥadiyya.
Manuscripts
مخطوطات
Over 30 mss. are listed by OY, of which six can be considered to be historic. The best is Shehit Ali 2813, which was copied by Ayyūb b. Badr and read in front of IA in Damascus in 621H. According to OY, the work was written in 601H in Jerusalem on the basis of a note on Veliyuddin 51, but our investigation shows that no date is given. (See Jane Clark and Denis McAuley, Some Notes on the Manuscript Veliyuddin 51, JMIAS, 40 (2006), pp. 101-116.)

Dating: The title K. al-Alif wa huwa k. al-ahadiyya is written on 19a, and underneath there is a note, which we believe is in the same hand, that translates as: Composed by Muhyî al-dîn ibn al-‘Arabî, who said (may God have mercy on him) “I composed it in Jerusalem (bayt al-muqaddas) in an hour in the daytime.” On 26a, at the end, a further note repeats this information and adds that the original was in Ibn ‘Arabî’s handwriting. Osman Yahia again concludes, on the basis of this second note, that the text was written in Jerusalem in 601,[10] but we could not find a date. The year 601 would be a reasonable conjecture, however, given the other notes in the collection. Title: Can be seen as two alternate titles or one full title...
